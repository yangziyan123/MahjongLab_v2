# Product

## Register

product

## Users

MahjongLab primarily serves serious self-study riichi mahjong players who want to improve through repeatable review and training. They are likely importing logs after a match, replaying key decisions, comparing their choices against AI recommendations, and saving recurring mistakes for focused practice. Their context is task-oriented: they want the product to make hard game analysis readable, searchable, and actionable without making them fight the interface.

Secondary audiences include training camp or community organizers, content creators, and future integration partners, but the default product decisions should optimize first for individual players building a disciplined improvement loop.

## Product Purpose

MahjongLab is an AI learning and training platform for riichi mahjong. It connects two core workflows: AI review of completed games and AI battle training that can feed directly into post-game analysis.

Success means a player can move from a raw game record or a just-finished training match to a clear understanding of important decision points, missed opportunities, recommended alternatives, and the next practice focus. The product should make review feel structured and trustworthy, not like a decorative report generator.

## Brand Personality

Concise, professional, premium, and quietly engaging. The interface should feel like a serious analytical tool made for players who care about precision, but it can use small, purposeful animations to make game state, progress, and feedback feel alive.

The voice should be direct and useful. It should avoid inflated AI-product language and avoid over-explaining obvious actions.

## Anti-references

Avoid generic AI-generated purple-blue gradients, excessive glassmorphism, nested cards, and flashy motion that does not explain state or improve workflow. Avoid landing-page aesthetics inside the app, ornamental dashboards, repetitive icon-card grids, and decorative effects that compete with mahjong decisions, report data, or replay comprehension.

## Design Principles

1. Keep the analysis in front. UI structure should reduce the distance between a decision, the AI recommendation, the reason it matters, and the next practice action.
2. Earn trust through restraint. Dense information, standard controls, stable layouts, and consistent component behavior matter more than visual novelty.
3. Make the learning loop visible. Import, review, replay, mistake collection, and training should feel like connected steps in one improvement system.
4. Use motion as feedback. Animation should clarify loading, state changes, replay progress, selection, and training transitions; it should not decorate static surfaces.
5. Respect serious players' time. Copy should be concise, labels should be specific, and primary paths should stay obvious even when reports contain a lot of data.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Default to WCAG AA-level contrast and keyboard-accessible controls. Important states such as deviation level, action quality, loading, and errors must not rely on color alone. Motion should respect reduced-motion preferences, and any animated feedback should remain understandable when motion is disabled.
